\subsection{Aliasing Test}\label{aliasing-test}}

The spectrum of the signal

\[a(t)=\lvert s(t)\rvert^2=\lvert\mathcal{F}^{-1}\left\{S(\omega)\right\}\rvert^2,\]

where \(\mathcal{F}\) denotes the Fourier transform operation, is given
by

\[A(\omega) = \mathcal{F}\left\{a(t)\right\} = \int S(\omega')S^*(\omega + \omega')\text{d}\omega'.\]

By direct substitution, it can be shown that \(A(\omega)=A^*(-\omega)\).

In any case, the expression for the spectrum suggests that the square
magnitude of a Nyquist-sampled signal is undersampled since the
correlation operation creates a bandwidth response that spans twice the
range of the original signal \(S(\omega)\).
